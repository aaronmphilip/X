§ 01 — System Overview

How the Algorithm Works

The For You feed is a multi-stage ML pipeline. Every post gets a single final score. Highest score wins placement.

final_score = Σ(weighti × P(actioni)) × author_diversity_multiplier × oon_factor
The Phoenix ML model predicts probabilities for 19 user actions → multiplied by weights → summed → adjusted for diversity and network

Pipeline Execution Order

1
Query Hydration
Fetch user context: following list, engagement history (last 128 actions), bloom filters for seen posts, demographic features, inferred topics, past request timestamps.
2
Candidate Sourcing — Parallel
Thunder pulls in-network posts from followed accounts in sub-millisecond. Phoenix retrieves out-of-network posts via two-tower ML similarity. Ads, Who-to-Follow, and Prompts sources run concurrently.
3
Candidate Hydration — Parallel
Enrich candidates with author metadata, engagement counts (fav, reply, repost, quote), video duration, media info, brand safety signals, language codes, subscription eligibility.
4
Pre-Selection Filtering — Sequential
Drop duplicates, self-posts, age violations, blocked/muted authors, muted keywords, previously seen posts (bloom filter + explicit IDs), paywalled content, ancillary visibility failures.
5
Scoring — Sequential
PhoenixScorer (ML predictions) → WeightedScorer (action weight combination) → AuthorDiversityScorer (decay multiplier) → OONScorer (out-of-network penalty).
6
Selection
BlenderSelector + TopKScoreSelector: sort all candidates by final score, select top K for the response.
7
Post-Selection Filtering — Sequential
VFFilter (visibility: spam, violence, gore, deleted), DedupConversationFilter (thread deduplication). These run after scoring — even high-scoring posts can be dropped here.
8
Side Effects — Async
Log served candidates to Kafka, publish seen post IDs, update Redis cache, update served history, update past request timestamps, log impressions.
§ 02 — Scoring Engine

The Weight Hierarchy

Phoenix predicts probabilities for 19 actions. Each gets multiplied by its weight. This is what you're actually optimizing for.

Rank Signal Weight Param Strategy
1 Follow Author FOLLOW_AUTHOR_WEIGHT Highest weight in the system. Design every post to make strangers want to follow you — not just like.
2 Reply REPLY_WEIGHT Replies beat likes in score contribution. Posts that generate conversation rank higher.
3 Quote QUOTE_WEIGHT High weight + brings entirely new audiences who generate their own engagement on your post.
4 Retweet / Repost RETWEET_WEIGHT High weight — reposts distribute your content to completely new follower graphs.
5 DM Share SHARE_VIA_DM_WEIGHT Heavily weighted and massively underused. "Send this to someone" is worth more than most realize.
6 Copy Link SHARE_VIA_COPY_LINK_WEIGHT Tracked even when shared outside X. Discord, WhatsApp, email shares register here if link is copied first.
7 Like / Favorite FAVORITE_WEIGHT Medium tier. Most creators over-optimize for likes when follows and replies matter more.
8 Video Quality View VQV_WEIGHT Medium — only fires if video exceeds MIN_VIDEO_DURATION_MS. Short videos get zero VQV weight.
9 Dwell Time DWELL_WEIGHT Medium — caps at exactly 30 seconds. Binary dwell + continuous dwell time both tracked separately.
10 Profile Click PROFILE_CLICK_WEIGHT Clicking your profile is tracked. Posts that make people curious about who you are drive this signal.
11 Photo Expand PHOTO_EXPAND_WEIGHT Tapping to expand an image is a tracked signal. Use images that reward zooming in.
— Not Interested NOT_INTERESTED_WEIGHT Negative signal. Applies ratio penalty formula — not a simple subtraction.
— Not Dwelled NOT_DWELLED_WEIGHT Negative signal for scroll-past. People skipping your post actively hurts score.
— Mute / Block / Report MUTE / BLOCK / REPORT Very negative. Uses NEGATIVE_WEIGHTS_SUM ratio penalty — not subtraction. One report wave tanks distribution for weeks.
⚠️
Negative signal formula: The algorithm uses a separate path for negative signals via NEGATIVE_WEIGHTS_SUM. They apply a ratio multiplier against your score — not a simple subtraction. One coordinated report/block wave can kneecap your distribution for weeks.
§ 03 — ML Model

What Phoenix Learns From You

The Phoenix transformer maintains a rolling history of your last 128 engagement actions and builds a mathematical embedding of your content identity.

For each action in your history, Phoenix encodes: which post, who the author was, what action you took, what surface you were on (home/search/topic/etc.), and how long you dwelled. It then finds people whose history embedding is most similar to your post's embedding — and serves your content to them.

Phoenix Mini Model — Source Code Config
# Transformer architecture
embedding_dimension    = 128
attention_heads        = 4
key_size_per_head      = 32
transformer_layers     = 4
widening_factor        = 2   # hidden = 128 × 2 = 256
history_seq_len        = 127  # last N user actions tracked
candidate_seq_len      = 64   # candidates ranked per batch

# Hash-based embeddings
num_user_hashes        = 2
num_item_hashes        = 2
num_author_hashes      = 2
vocab_size             = 1_000_000  # per user/item/author

# Dwell normalization
norm_scale             = 30.0  # cap at 30 seconds
num_actions            = 19    # predicted action types
💡
Candidate Isolation: Candidates in the ranking batch cannot attend to each other in the transformer — only self-attention. This means your post's score is computed independently of what else is in the batch. Scores are deterministic and batch-size-independent.

Why Topic Consistency Matters

The two-tower retrieval model builds your author embedding from your entire posting history. If you post about 5 different topics, the model doesn't know which users to route you to. A consistent topic produces a tight, high-confidence embedding that the model routes accurately to pre-qualified audiences.

USER TOWER
Encodes the viewer's 127-action engagement history into a user embedding vector.
CANDIDATE TOWER
Projects your post + author embeddings into the same shared vector space.
SIMILARITY MATCH
Dot product between user and candidate vectors → top-K retrieved for ranking.
COLD START
New users go to a different cluster with topic-based retrieval until history builds up.
§ 04 — Author Diversity Scorer

Author Diversity Decay

The most counterintuitive mechanic. Posting more per day actively hurts each post's score within the same feed load.

AuthorDiversityScorer — Source Code Formula
multiplier(position) = (1.0 - floor) × decay_factor^position + floor

# With decay=0.9, floor=0.4:
1st post from author in feed load:  1.00×  (full score)
2nd post from same author:          0.94×
3rd post from same author:          0.89×
4th post from same author:          0.84×
# ...floors permanently at:        0.40×
⚡
The decay resets per feed load. Every time a user opens the app or refreshes, the author decay counter resets for all authors. This means spacing posts by 3+ hours ensures each post enters a new feed load at 1.0× multiplier instead of 0.94× or lower.

Practical Rules

  • Posting 3× per day means posts 2 and 3 are competing at 0.94× and 0.89× in any single feed load
  • One strong post per session beats three mediocre ones every time
  • Minimum 3-hour gap between posts to hit fresh feed loads
  • This decay is per-user, not global — each viewer's feed applies it independently
§ 05 — OON Scorer

Out-of-Network Scoring

How you reach people who don't follow you — and why early engagement is structurally required to make it work.

# OONScorer — applied after WeightedScorer
if !in_network:
    score *= OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR  # a fraction < 1.0

# New users get a more generous factor:
if follower_count < threshold:
    score *= NEW_USER_OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR  # higher than OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR
✅
New Account Advantage: Small accounts get a higher OON weight via NEW_USER_OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR. The algorithm is explicitly more generous with discovery for accounts with few followers. This is your best growth window — the OON penalty is smallest when you're starting out.

To beat the OON penalty your post needs a much higher base score than in-network posts. This is why viral posts need critical mass early — they need enough engagement signal to overcome the OON penalty and still rank highly for strangers. Seeding early engagement is not optional; it's structurally required to reach non-followers.

§ 06 — Candidate Retrieval

How You Enter the Candidate Pool

Before scoring even begins, your post must be retrieved. There are two completely separate paths.

Thunder — In-Network Store

Real-time Kafka-based per-user post store. Your posts automatically appear here for every follower at sub-millisecond latency. Handles original posts, replies, reposts, and video posts in separate stores per user. Posts auto-trimmed after retention period.

Phoenix — Out-of-Network Two-Tower Model

ML similarity model. Your post only appears as an OON candidate if its embedding is similar enough to a viewer's history. New users route to PhoenixRetrievalNewUserInferenceClusterId using topic-based retrieval until they have enough history. Trending topics = entry into this pipeline.

Other Candidate Sources

The pipeline runs multiple sources in parallel: PhoenixMoeSource (mixture of experts variant), PhoenixTopicsSource (topic-based retrieval), CachedPostsSource, PushToHomeSource, AdsSource, WhoToFollowSource, PromptsSource.

💡
A/B routing: Decider flags like enable_phoenix_retrieval_lap7_to_fou and enable_phoenix_retrieval_fou_to_lap7 allow real-time cluster switching for experiments. The algorithm is constantly A/B testing your content routing.
§ 07 — Post Age

The 80-Hour Cliff

After 80 hours, your post exits the retrieval window entirely. No slow trickle — a hard cliff.

# Phoenix model — post age bucketing
POST_AGE_MAX_MINUTES = 4800   # 80 hours
granularity_mins     = 60     # bucket every hour
num_buckets          = 82     # 81 normal + 1 overflow + 1 missing

# Posts older than 80 hours → overflow bucket → not retrieved
⏰
Hard deadline: All promotion, seeding, and engagement must happen within 80 hours. There is no organic rediscovery of old posts in the For You feed. The first 6 hours matter most — maximum engagement velocity early seeds OON distribution for the remaining 74 hours.

What This Means

  • Post when your most engaged followers are online so they seed the algorithm fast
  • Every hour of delayed engagement is an hour of diminishing distribution window
  • Don't post and disappear — be active for the first 2-3 hours after posting to respond to replies
  • There is no such thing as a post that "blows up later" unless it's being deliberately promoted
§ 08 — Spam Classifier

Spam Filter Tiers

The SpamEapiLowFollowerClassifier applies different scrutiny levels based on follower count. Getting to 1,000 is your single most important early goal.

# SpamEapiLowFollowerClassifier — follower tier categories
lte_100   -- Both users <=100 followers  →  HIGHEST scrutiny
lte_500   -- Both users <=500             →  High scrutiny
lte_1000  -- Both users <=1000            →  Elevated scrutiny
gt_1000   -- At least one user >1000      →  LOWEST scrutiny
⚡
The 1,000 threshold changes everything. Under 1,000 followers, your content AND all your engagements are scrutinized more aggressively. Getting to 1,000+ unlocks the lower-scrutiny tier. This single milestone changes how the entire system treats you.

Engaging with other small accounts (both <100 followers) puts you in the maximum scrutiny tier for that interaction. Engaging with accounts over 1,000 followers puts you in the gt_1000 tier — lower scrutiny. This is why getting into conversations with established accounts matters beyond just exposure.

§ 11 — Dwell Signals

Dwell Time — The 30-Second Rule

The model tracks both whether you paused and how long. But the curve maxes at exactly 30 seconds.

# Phoenix model — continuous dwell normalization
norm_scale: float = 30.0
normalized = clip(dwell_value, 0, 30) / 30.0

# 0s  → 0.0  (no signal)
# 15s → 0.5  (half signal)
# 30s → 1.0  (maximum signal)
# 60s → 1.0  (same as 30s — no additional benefit)

# Two separate signals tracked:
DWELL_WEIGHT           # binary: paused at all?
CONT_DWELL_TIME_WEIGHT # continuous: how long?
The Optimal Post Length

A post that takes 25-30 seconds to read and satisfies the reader earns the same dwell score as a 3-minute essay. The dwell ceiling is 30 seconds. Design for that — not more. Hook in the first line. Deliver in the body. Done at 30 seconds.

§ 12 — Video Scoring

Video & The VQV Gate

Short videos lose an entire scoring category. Video only helps if you clear the minimum duration threshold.

# WeightedScorer — VQV eligibility check
fn vqv_weight_eligibility(candidate) -> f64 {
    if candidate.min_video_duration_ms > MIN_VIDEO_DURATION_MS {
        return VQV_WEIGHT  // full weight applied
    }
    return 0.0            // weight nulled out
}

# Also: QUOTED_VQV_WEIGHT for videos in quoted posts
# EnableQuotedVqvDurationCheck flag controls quoted video VQV
⚡
Binary gate: A video slightly below MIN_VIDEO_DURATION_MS gets zero VQV weight — not reduced weight. The entire scoring category is nulled. Commit to a length above the threshold or don't rely on video for VQV signal.

Video that qualifies for VQV earns signal on top of all other engagement actions — likes, replies, DMs, follows still all fire independently. A qualifying video with strong engagement is the highest-ceiling format in the algorithm when executed well.

§ 13 — Phoenix Actions

The 19-Action Model

Every action Phoenix predicts — including the ones most creators have never thought about.

follow_author_score
Highest weight. The entire algorithm optimizes for this outcome above all others.
reply_score
Second most powerful. Posts that start conversations rank far above passive-like posts.
share_via_dm_score
Heavily weighted and almost universally ignored by creators. Engineer posts for "send this to someone."
share_via_copy_link_score
Tracks external sharing. Discord, WhatsApp, email shares register if link was copied.

High weight. Gets you in front of the quoter's entire audience who then generate their own engagement.

High weight. Amplifies your post to a completely new follower graph.
photo_expand_score
Tapping to zoom an image is tracked. Use images that reward expanding — charts, infographics, screenshots.
profile_click_score
Clicking your profile from the post is tracked. Posts that create "who is this?" curiosity drive this.
vqv_score
Video quality view. Only activates on videos above MIN_VIDEO_DURATION_MS threshold.
dwell_score + dwell_time
Binary dwell (paused at all) + continuous dwell time. Both tracked. Both cap at 30 seconds of benefit.
click_score
Link clicks tracked separately. Click dwell time (how long spent after clicking) is also a continuous signal.
favorite_score

Negative weight. Applies ratio penalty via NEGATIVE_WEIGHTS_SUM formula.
not_dwelled_score
Scroll-past is actively negative. People skipping hurts your score.
mute_author_score
Negative. Accumulation over time tanks your author embedding quality.
block_author_score
Very negative. One coordinated block wave can suppress distribution for weeks.
report_score
Most negative. Never post content that could trigger coordinated reports even if it might perform well.
§ 14 — Common Myths

Formatting, Lowercase, Articles, Communities

What the source code actually says about formatting choices — and which popular beliefs are myths.

MYTH: Lowercase Text Boosts Reach

Zero mention of letter case anywhere in the algorithm code. Phoenix represents posts as hash embeddings — it does not parse sentences. The "lowercase boosts reach" claim has no algorithmic basis. Letter case is completely invisible to the scoring system. Any effect is indirect: lowercase may feel more authentic to some audiences → better human engagement → indirect score improvement.

Articles / Long-form

No dedicated article boost in the scoring code. The product surface enum has 16 values but no special "article" multiplier. Long-form naturally earns more dwell time, and if the content converts readers to followers it's worth it. Otherwise a shorter post with equivalent engagement signals scores the same.

Communities

No dedicated weight multiplier for community posts in the scoring code. However, community audiences are pre-qualified for specific topics → naturally higher engagement rates → better ML predictions → higher predicted action probabilities. The real benefit: communities concentrate your engagement in a specific topic, strengthening your author embedding and improving For You routing for all your regular posts over time.

Line Breaks, Spacing, Emojis

Invisible to the scoring system. Affects human behavior → dwell and engagement → indirect score effect. Line breaks that make posts scannable cause readers to pause longer → dwell signal fires. Emojis have zero algorithmic value but may affect whether humans stop scrolling past your post.

§ 15 — Virality Mechanics

The Banger System

There is literally a system called BangerInitialScreen in the source code. It's a Grok VLM classifier that decides whether your post deserves viral amplification.

# grox/classifiers/content/banger_initial_screen.py
class BangerInitialScreenClassifier(ContentClassifier):
    vlm_config.temperature = 0.000001  # near-zero = deterministic

# BangerInitialScreenResult fields:
quality_score:        float         # 0.0 to 1.0
description:          str           # Grok's summary of post
tags:                 list[str]     # topic tags assigned
taxonomy_categories:  list[dict]    # internal topic taxonomy
slop_score:           int | None    # 1, 2, or 3
has_minor_score:      float | None
tweet_bool_metadata:               # isHighQuality, isNsfw, isGore...

# The hardcoded threshold:
banger_initial_positive = score >= 0.4
⚡
Deterministic: Temperature = 0.000001 means the same post gets the same score every time. There is no luck involved. The Grok VLM reads your full post + all images + your user profile + current trending topics and produces a fixed quality score.

The Tweet Bool Metadata Flags

Every post that passes through the banger system gets permanently annotated with these boolean flags stored in unified post annotations:

FlagEffect

isNsfwRestricts distribution. Triggers record_nsfw_detection(post).
isGoreHard distribution block.
isViolentHard distribution block.
isSpamDrops post from For You entirely.
isSoftNsfwRestricted distribution.
isAdultRestricted to opted-in users only.
§ 16 — The Gate

The Min Traction Gate

The most important mechanic most people don't know exists. The Banger classifier only runs on posts that already crossed a minimum engagement threshold.

Every published post → CONTENT_UNDERSTANDING_REALTIME_UNIFIED_POSTS ALL POSTS
After min traction → UNIFIED_POSTS_MIN_TRACTION_FOR_GROX BANGER SCREEN
Min traction + safety → UNIFIED_POSTS_MIN_TRACTION_FOR_GROX_PTOS SAFETY SCREEN
Min traction + media → UNIFIED_POSTS_MIN_TRACTION_FOR_GROX_MULTI_MODAL MULTIMODAL
🔑
The gate: Without crossing min traction, the banger classifier never sees your post. It doesn't matter how good your content is — if it doesn't get initial engagement, it never gets screened for viral amplification. The first 30-60 minutes are everything.

How to Cross the Threshold

  • DM the post to 5-10 people who will genuinely engage — fastest way to seed min traction without looking like spam
  • Get 2-3 real replies fast — reply weight is 2nd highest action weight, making early replies particularly valuable
  • Engage other posts immediately before posting — drives profile visits that convert to early views
  • Have your most engaged followers ready — they provide the cleanest traction signal for the algorithm
§ 17 — Quality Detection

The Slop Score

X uses Grok to detect and suppress generic, AI-generated, or templated content. Being broad and safe is literally scored against you.

Generic motivational content ("work hard, stay focused")
AI-generated text with no specific viewpoint or data
Rephrased takes everyone already posted
Content without a unique angle or perspective
Template-style or formulaic posts
Vague observations with no concrete claim
✅ Low Slop — Amplified
Specific claims with concrete details or data

Most counterintuitive finding in the codebase: The system has an explicit anti-generic-content classifier running on every post that gains traction. Specificity wins. A post with a controversial specific claim scores better than a broadly agreeable platitude.
§ 18 — Vision Language Model

VLM — What It Is and Why It Affects You

VLM = Vision Language Model. It reads your image and text simultaneously and evaluates them as a single combined piece of content.

Regular language models only read text. The Grok VLM sees your image + caption + user profile all at once. It evaluates the combination — not the parts separately. This has major implications for how you use images.

What the VLM Receives Per Post
# BangerInitialScreenClassifier.build_convo()
system_msg: BangerMiniVlmScreenScore.render(topics=trending_topics)
user_msg:   UserRenderer.render(post.user)   # follower count, age, verification
            + PostRenderer.render(post)        # text + all media

# Trending topics fetched every hour:
cls._cached_topics = StratoGrokTopics().fetch()
CACHE_TTL_SECONDS  = 3600

How Images Affect Your Quality Score

Image TypeEffect on quality_score

Original chart or screenshot proving your claim↑ Best case — highest score
Dense infographic requiring zoom to read↑ Quality boost + PHOTO_EXPAND signal

AI-generated generic image↓ VLM likely recognizes it, raises slop score

💡
Trending topics injection: The VLM is explicitly given the current Grok topic taxonomy (refreshed hourly). It's asking: "does this post relate to what's trending right now?" Posts that map to trending taxonomy categories get amplified into those topic feeds. Not hashtags — the VLM reads actual topic relevance from your content.
§ 19 — The Full Loop

The Viral Flywheel

The complete path from publishing to going viral — every step derived from source code.

1
Post Published
Enters CONTENT_UNDERSTANDING_REALTIME_UNIFIED_POSTS stream. ML embeddings generated immediately. Thunder serves to your followers at sub-millisecond latency.
2
In-Network Distribution Begins
Your followers see the post. Their engagement quality determines how the algorithm routes the post to strangers. High-quality early engagement = better OON routing.
3
Manufacture Min Traction — First 30-60 Minutes
DM the post to 5-10 people. Get 2-3 real replies fast. Drive profile visits. Cross the min traction threshold — this is the gate to everything downstream.
4
Grok VLM Analysis
Post enters MIN_TRACTION_FOR_GROX stream. Grok VLM analyzes at temperature=0.000001 (deterministic). Assigns quality_score, slop_score, tags, taxonomy categories. This analysis is permanent.
5
Banger Screen Result
If quality_score ≥ 0.4: banger_initial_positive = TRUE. The isHighQuality boolean is stored permanently in unified post annotations. This is a persistent ranking signal for the post's full 80-hour lifespan.
6
OON Distribution Unlocks
With isHighQuality = true, the post gets served to out-of-network users following related taxonomy categories. The OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR penalty is partially offset by the quality annotation.
7
The Cascade
Stranger engagement feeds back into Phoenix real-time scoring. Higher predicted action probabilities → higher scores in next feed loads → more OON distribution → more engagement. The flywheel spins.
8
Author Embedding Updated
The viral engagement permanently updates your author embedding. Future posts get better routing to the audiences that engaged with this post. Each viral post makes the next one easier.
⏰
80-Hour Hard Cliff
Post exits retrieval window entirely. All of the above must happen within 80 hours. After that, the post is functionally dead for For You distribution.
§ 20 — The Playbook

Three stages, each with a different algorithmic context and different optimal tactics.

Stage 1: 100 → 1,000

2
Reply to large accounts in your niche aggressively. Your reply is a separate scored candidate in other people's For You feeds. Engagement on your reply = out-of-network impressions with zero follower base.

Optimize every post for FOLLOW_AUTHOR_WEIGHT, not likes. Posts where a stranger thinks "who is this, I want more" — cliffhangers, unexpected angles, posts that feel like the first chapter of something.

6
Post within trending conversations. New users route through topic-based retrieval (EnableNewUserTopicRetrieval). Trending topics are your fastest entry into this discovery pipeline as a small account.
7

13
Engineer DM-worthy posts specifically. "Send this to someone who needs to hear this" framing directly targets SHARE_VIA_DM_WEIGHT. The most underused high-weight signal on the platform by a wide margin.
