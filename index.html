§ 01 — System Overview

How the Algorithm Works

The For You feed is a multi-stage ML pipeline. Every post gets a single final score. Highest score wins placement.

final_score = Σ(weighti × P(actioni)) × author_diversity_multiplier × oon_factor
The Phoenix ML model predicts probabilities for 19 user actions → multiplied by weights → summed → adjusted for diversity and network

Pipeline Execution Order

1
Query Hydration
Fetch user context: following list, engagement history (last 128 actions), bloom filters for seen posts, demographic features, inferred topics, past request timestamps.
2
Candidate Sourcing — Parallel
Thunder pulls in-network posts from followed accounts in sub-millisecond. Phoenix retrieves out-of-network posts via two-tower ML similarity. Ads, Who-to-Follow, and Prompts sources run concurrently.
3
Candidate Hydration — Parallel
Enrich candidates with author metadata, engagement counts (fav, reply, repost, quote), video duration, media info, brand safety signals, language codes, subscription eligibility.
4
Pre-Selection Filtering — Sequential
Drop duplicates, self-posts, age violations, blocked/muted authors, muted keywords, previously seen posts (bloom filter + explicit IDs), paywalled content, ancillary visibility failures.
5
Scoring — Sequential
PhoenixScorer (ML predictions) → WeightedScorer (action weight combination) → AuthorDiversityScorer (decay multiplier) → OONScorer (out-of-network penalty).
6
Selection
BlenderSelector + TopKScoreSelector: sort all candidates by final score, select top K for the response.
7
Post-Selection Filtering — Sequential
VFFilter (visibility: spam, violence, gore, deleted), DedupConversationFilter (thread deduplication). These run after scoring — even high-scoring posts can be dropped here.
8
Side Effects — Async
Log served candidates to Kafka, publish seen post IDs, update Redis cache, update served history, update past request timestamps, log impressions.
§ 02 — Scoring Engine

The Weight Hierarchy

Phoenix predicts probabilities for 19 actions. Each gets multiplied by its weight. This is what you're actually optimizing for.

Rank Signal Weight Param Strategy
1 Follow Author FOLLOW_AUTHOR_WEIGHT Highest weight in the system. Design every post to make strangers want to follow you — not just like.
2 Reply REPLY_WEIGHT Replies beat likes in score contribution. Posts that generate conversation rank higher.
3 Quote QUOTE_WEIGHT High weight + brings entirely new audiences who generate their own engagement on your post.
4 Retweet / Repost RETWEET_WEIGHT High weight — reposts distribute your content to completely new follower graphs.
5 DM Share SHARE_VIA_DM_WEIGHT Heavily weighted and massively underused. "Send this to someone" is worth more than most realize.
6 Copy Link SHARE_VIA_COPY_LINK_WEIGHT Tracked even when shared outside X. Discord, WhatsApp, email shares register here if link is copied first.
7 Like / Favorite FAVORITE_WEIGHT Medium tier. Most creators over-optimize for likes when follows and replies matter more.
8 Video Quality View VQV_WEIGHT Medium — only fires if video exceeds MIN_VIDEO_DURATION_MS. Short videos get zero VQV weight.
9 Dwell Time DWELL_WEIGHT Medium — caps at exactly 30 seconds. Binary dwell + continuous dwell time both tracked separately.
10 Profile Click PROFILE_CLICK_WEIGHT Clicking your profile is tracked. Posts that make people curious about who you are drive this signal.
11 Photo Expand PHOTO_EXPAND_WEIGHT Tapping to expand an image is a tracked signal. Use images that reward zooming in.
— Not Interested NOT_INTERESTED_WEIGHT Negative signal. Applies ratio penalty formula — not a simple subtraction.
— Not Dwelled NOT_DWELLED_WEIGHT Negative signal for scroll-past. People skipping your post actively hurts score.
— Mute / Block / Report MUTE / BLOCK / REPORT Very negative. Uses NEGATIVE_WEIGHTS_SUM ratio penalty — not subtraction. One report wave tanks distribution for weeks.
⚠️
Negative signal formula: The algorithm uses a separate path for negative signals via NEGATIVE_WEIGHTS_SUM. They apply a ratio multiplier against your score — not a simple subtraction. One coordinated report/block wave can kneecap your distribution for weeks.
§ 03 — ML Model

What Phoenix Learns From You

The Phoenix transformer maintains a rolling history of your last 128 engagement actions and builds a mathematical embedding of your content identity.

For each action in your history, Phoenix encodes: which post, who the author was, what action you took, what surface you were on (home/search/topic/etc.), and how long you dwelled. It then finds people whose history embedding is most similar to your post's embedding — and serves your content to them.

Phoenix Mini Model — Source Code Config
# Transformer architecture
embedding_dimension    = 128
attention_heads        = 4
key_size_per_head      = 32
transformer_layers     = 4
widening_factor        = 2   # hidden = 128 × 2 = 256
history_seq_len        = 127  # last N user actions tracked
candidate_seq_len      = 64   # candidates ranked per batch

# Hash-based embeddings
num_user_hashes        = 2
num_item_hashes        = 2
num_author_hashes      = 2
vocab_size             = 1_000_000  # per user/item/author

# Dwell normalization
norm_scale             = 30.0  # cap at 30 seconds
num_actions            = 19    # predicted action types
💡
Candidate Isolation: Candidates in the ranking batch cannot attend to each other in the transformer — only self-attention. This means your post's score is computed independently of what else is in the batch. Scores are deterministic and batch-size-independent.

Why Topic Consistency Matters

The two-tower retrieval model builds your author embedding from your entire posting history. If you post about 5 different topics, the model doesn't know which users to route you to. A consistent topic produces a tight, high-confidence embedding that the model routes accurately to pre-qualified audiences.

USER TOWER
Encodes the viewer's 127-action engagement history into a user embedding vector.
CANDIDATE TOWER
Projects your post + author embeddings into the same shared vector space.
SIMILARITY MATCH
Dot product between user and candidate vectors → top-K retrieved for ranking.
COLD START
New users go to a different cluster with topic-based retrieval until history builds up.
§ 04 — Author Diversity Scorer

Author Diversity Decay

The most counterintuitive mechanic. Posting more per day actively hurts each post's score within the same feed load.

AuthorDiversityScorer — Source Code Formula
multiplier(position) = (1.0 - floor) × decay_factor^position + floor

# With decay=0.9, floor=0.4:
1st post from author in feed load:  1.00×  (full score)
2nd post from same author:          0.94×
3rd post from same author:          0.89×
4th post from same author:          0.84×
# ...floors permanently at:        0.40×
⚡
The decay resets per feed load. Every time a user opens the app or refreshes, the author decay counter resets for all authors. This means spacing posts by 3+ hours ensures each post enters a new feed load at 1.0× multiplier instead of 0.94× or lower.

Practical Rules

  • Posting 3× per day means posts 2 and 3 are competing at 0.94× and 0.89× in any single feed load
  • One strong post per session beats three mediocre ones every time
  • Minimum 3-hour gap between posts to hit fresh feed loads
  • This decay is per-user, not global — each viewer's feed applies it independently
§ 04b — Secondary Ranker

VM Ranker & DPP Diversity Re-Ranking

After Phoenix scores every candidate, a second model runs — the Value Model Ranker — that can completely reorder the feed using a Determinantal Point Process to enforce diversity.

Most creators don't know this exists. The Phoenix score gets you into the feed. The VM Ranker decides where in the feed you actually land. It applies DPP (Determinantal Point Process) — a mathematical model that intentionally penalises similar content clustering together.

# VM Ranker params (home-mixer/scorers/vm_ranker.rs)
EnableVMRanker             = true         # feature-switch gated
VMRankerValueModelId       = "<model_id>"  # secondary model

# DPP diversity enforcement
VMRankerDppTheta           = float        # how strongly diversity is enforced
VMRankerDppMaxSelectedRank = int          # max rank positions affected by DPP

# Inputs to VM Ranker:
phoenix_scores             # from PhoenixScorer
viewer_following_count     # viewer's social graph size
new_user_age_threshold_secs # whether viewer is "new user"
⚡
What DPP means for you: If you've already appeared multiple times in a viewer's recent feed loads, the DPP diversity model actively penalises showing you again — even if your Phoenix score is high. This is a second layer of author diversity on top of the decay multiplier. You cannot spam your way into high placement even if your base score is excellent.

Practical Implications

  • One great post beats five average ones — the DPP model will de-rank your 3rd and 4th posts in a session regardless of their raw Phoenix score
  • Spacing posts by 24h+ allows DPP diversity scores to reset, giving each post a cleaner slate
  • High Phoenix score still matters first — DPP only re-orders candidates that are already in the selection pool; if your score doesn't get you in the pool, DPP is irrelevant
  • The DPP theta param controls how aggressively diversity is enforced — this is tuned platform-wide, not per-user
§ 05 — OON Scorer

Out-of-Network Scoring

How you reach people who don't follow you — and why early engagement is structurally required to make it work.

# OONScorer — applied after WeightedScorer
if !in_network:
    score *= OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR  # a fraction < 1.0

# New users get a more generous factor:
if follower_count < threshold:
    score *= NEW_USER_OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR  # higher than OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR
✅
New Account Advantage: Small accounts get a higher OON weight via NEW_USER_OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR. The algorithm is explicitly more generous with discovery for accounts with few followers. This is your best growth window — the OON penalty is smallest when you're starting out.

To beat the OON penalty your post needs a much higher base score than in-network posts. This is why viral posts need critical mass early — they need enough engagement signal to overcome the OON penalty and still rank highly for strangers. Seeding early engagement is not optional; it's structurally required to reach non-followers.

§ 05b — Network Signals

Network Signals: Jaccard Similarity & Topic OON

Two hidden multipliers that most creators never optimise for — your network overlap with viewers, and a separate OON weight when you enter topic feeds.

Mutual Follow Jaccard Similarity
# mutual_follow_jaccard_hydrator.rs
signal: mutual_follow_jaccard
method: Jaccard similarity between viewer follower minhash
         AND author follower minhash
required: >= 256 minhash signatures to be valid
# Higher overlap → higher signal → post boosted to that viewer

The algorithm measures what fraction of your followers also follow the viewer. If you and a viewer share many mutual followers, your posts are ranked higher for them.

💡
How to use this: Engage heavily with the accounts your target audience follows. When you and a viewer both follow accounts A, B, and C, your Jaccard score with that viewer rises. This means community participation — replying in the same conversations your target audience is in — directly raises your algorithmic visibility to them, even before they follow you.
Topic OON Weight Factor — Separate from Standard OON
# home-mixer/scorers/oon_scorer.rs
if !in_network AND topic_request_active:
    score *= TopicOonWeightFactor   # different from OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR

if !in_network AND !topic_request:
    score *= OonWeightFactor        # standard OON penalty

When your post enters a topic/trending feed (via VLM taxonomy match), it uses a different OON weight than general For You distribution. This factor can be more generous — meaning trending topic posts face a lighter OON penalty than generic OON posts.

✅
The implication: Getting the VLM to assign your post to a trending taxonomy category isn't just about topic feed traffic — it also reduces your OON penalty in the main For You feed for those viewers. Two wins from one mechanic. Write posts that the VLM can confidently tag to a specific trending topic.
§ 06 — Candidate Retrieval

How You Enter the Candidate Pool

Before scoring even begins, your post must be retrieved. There are two completely separate paths.

Thunder — In-Network Store

Real-time Kafka-based per-user post store. Your posts automatically appear here for every follower at sub-millisecond latency. Handles original posts, replies, reposts, and video posts in separate stores per user. Posts auto-trimmed after retention period.

Phoenix — Out-of-Network Two-Tower Model

ML similarity model. Your post only appears as an OON candidate if its embedding is similar enough to a viewer's history. New users route to PhoenixRetrievalNewUserInferenceClusterId using topic-based retrieval until they have enough history. Trending topics = entry into this pipeline.

Other Candidate Sources

The pipeline runs multiple sources in parallel: PhoenixMoeSource (mixture of experts variant), PhoenixTopicsSource (topic-based retrieval), CachedPostsSource, PushToHomeSource, AdsSource, WhoToFollowSource, PromptsSource.

💡
A/B routing: Decider flags like enable_phoenix_retrieval_lap7_to_fou and enable_phoenix_retrieval_fou_to_lap7 allow real-time cluster switching for experiments. The algorithm is constantly A/B testing your content routing.
§ 07 — Post Age

The 80-Hour Cliff

After 80 hours, your post exits the retrieval window entirely. No slow trickle — a hard cliff.

# Phoenix model — post age bucketing
POST_AGE_MAX_MINUTES = 4800   # 80 hours
granularity_mins     = 60     # bucket every hour
num_buckets          = 82     # 81 normal + 1 overflow + 1 missing

# Posts older than 80 hours → overflow bucket → not retrieved
⏰
Hard deadline: All promotion, seeding, and engagement must happen within 80 hours. There is no organic rediscovery of old posts in the For You feed. The first 6 hours matter most — maximum engagement velocity early seeds OON distribution for the remaining 74 hours.

What This Means

  • Post when your most engaged followers are online so they seed the algorithm fast
  • Every hour of delayed engagement is an hour of diminishing distribution window
  • Don't post and disappear — be active for the first 2-3 hours after posting to respond to replies
  • There is no such thing as a post that "blows up later" unless it's being deliberately promoted
§ 07b — Post Age Velocity

Age Velocity Windows Within the 80 Hours

The 80-hour cliff isn't a flat window. The algorithm tracks distinct age buckets — meaning engagement in the first 30 minutes is measured separately from the 1-hour window, the 6-hour window, and beyond.

# tweet_type_metrics_hydrator.rs — age bucket flags
TWEET_AGE_LTE_30_MINUTES   # 0–30 min: highest velocity window
TWEET_AGE_LTE_1_HOUR       # 0–1 hr
TWEET_AGE_LTE_6_HOURS      # 0–6 hrs: primary OON distribution window
TWEET_AGE_LTE_12_HOURS     # 0–12 hrs
TWEET_AGE_GTE_24_HOURS     # 24h+: long tail window
0 – 30 MINUTES
Critical velocity window. Posts that accumulate engagement fastest here get priority OON routing. Every minute matters.
1 – 6 HOURS
Primary OON distribution window. If min traction is crossed and VLM scores ≥0.4, this is when viral amplification fires.
6 – 24 HOURS
Sustained distribution. Posts still in flight here are actively accruing OON impressions. Reply velocity still counts.
24 – 80 HOURS
Long tail. Engagement still registered but distribution slows. After 80h: hard exit from the retrieval window.
⚠️
The real insight: The algorithm doesn't just know your post is "young" — it knows exactly which velocity bucket you're in. A post that gets 50 replies in 30 minutes is classified differently from one that gets 50 replies in 6 hours. Velocity of engagement is a first-class signal, not just volume. Front-load as much real engagement as possible into the first 30-minute window.
§ 08 — Spam Classifier

Spam Filter Tiers

The SpamEapiLowFollowerClassifier applies different scrutiny levels based on follower count. Getting to 1,000 is your single most important early goal.

# SpamEapiLowFollowerClassifier — follower tier categories
lte_100   -- Both users <=100 followers  →  HIGHEST scrutiny
lte_500   -- Both users <=500             →  High scrutiny
lte_1000  -- Both users <=1000            →  Elevated scrutiny
gt_1000   -- At least one user >1000      →  LOWEST scrutiny
⚡
The 1,000 threshold changes everything. Under 1,000 followers, your content AND all your engagements are scrutinized more aggressively. Getting to 1,000+ unlocks the lower-scrutiny tier. This single milestone changes how the entire system treats you.

Engaging with other small accounts (both <100 followers) puts you in the maximum scrutiny tier for that interaction. Engaging with accounts over 1,000 followers puts you in the gt_1000 tier — lower scrutiny. This is why getting into conversations with established accounts matters beyond just exposure.

§ 08c — New User Mode

New User Mode — Separate Clusters & Higher OON

Brand-new accounts don't go through the same Phoenix pipeline as established ones. They route to entirely separate inference clusters with different weights.

# home-mixer — new user routing params
NewUserAgeThresholdSecs              # account age cutoff for "new user"
NEW_USER_MIN_FOLLOWING               # min follows needed to exit new-user mode
PhoenixRankerNewUserInferenceClusterId   # separate ranker cluster
PhoenixRetrievalNewUserInferenceClusterId # separate retrieval cluster
NEW_USER_OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR           # higher than standard OON_WEIGHT_FACTOR
EnableNewUserTopicRetrieval          # topic-based retrieval for new users
✅
The new account window is real and measurable. While in new-user mode: your OON weight is higher (less OON penalty), you get topic-based retrieval (trending topics route you to interested audiences immediately), and you're evaluated on a separate ranker cluster tuned for account bootstrapping. This window closes once your account age and following count exceed the thresholds. It is the single best period to grow aggressively.

How to Maximise New User Mode

  • Post into trending topics immediately — EnableNewUserTopicRetrieval is active; the algorithm will match your posts to topic audiences before you have followers to seed engagement
  • Follow accounts in your niche fast — NEW_USER_MIN_FOLLOWING is part of the exit condition; paradoxically, following more people helps build the engagement history Phoenix needs to route you accurately
  • Don't waste this window on low-effort posts — higher OON weight means more reach per post; each post during new-user mode has structurally more distribution potential than it will after the window closes
  • The window is account-age-gated — it closes automatically regardless of growth. Use it fully from day one.

§ 10b — Feed Deduplication

Conversation Deduplication

Only one post per conversation thread appears in any user's For You feed — the highest-scoring one. This changes how threads, reply chains, and quote stacks work strategically.

# dedup_conversation_filter.rs
group_by: conversation_id   # root of the reply chain
keep:     highest_score      # only the top scorer per conversation
drop:     all others         # even high-scoring duplicates removed

# retweet_deduplication_filter.rs
dedup_key: retweeted_tweet_id # if retweet; else tweet_id
keep:      first occurrence   # first seen wins
⚡
Thread strategy rewrite: If you post a thread where post 3 goes viral, post 3 is the one that enters For You feeds — not post 1. But post 3 still links back to your full thread. This means each thread post must be independently compelling, not just a continuation. Never make thread post 1 a weak hook that depends on later posts for context.
❌ Wrong Thread Strategy
Make post 1 a teaser ("a thread on X...")
Rely on post 1 to hook readers into later posts
Put your best insight at post 5 or 6
Reply to your own thread immediately (same convo)
✅ Correct Thread Strategy
Make every post in the thread standalone-valuable
Put the highest-value insight early (post 1 or 2)
Let the best post pull readers to the full thread
Space out major insights to separate conversation roots
§ 10c — Traction Mechanics

Engagement Velocity & Count Hydration

Real-time engagement counts are fetched on a TTL-based schedule — new posts are checked more frequently. How fast traction builds is treated as a distinct signal from total traction.

# engagement_counts_hydrator.rs — TTL strategy
new_tweet_ttl    = 5   minutes  # checked every 5 min when fresh
old_tweet_ttl    = 10  minutes  # checked every 10 min after threshold
age_threshold    = 30  minutes  # pivot from "new" to "old" TTL
EnableContextFeatures = true    # gates real-time count inclusion

# Counts hydrated: fav_count, reply_count, repost_count, quote_count
🔑
The 30-minute pivot: For the first 30 minutes, the system re-fetches your engagement counts every 5 minutes. After 30 minutes, it switches to 10-minute intervals. This means engagement that lands in the first 5-minute window after posting has an outsized effect — it's the freshest signal snapshot the algorithm has. The very first replies, likes, and reposts matter disproportionately.
0 – 5 MINUTES
First TTL window. Engagement here is captured in the algorithm's very first count snapshot. Highest signal freshness.
5 – 30 MINUTES
Still on 5-minute TTL. Each snapshot update can trigger re-scoring and improved OON routing if counts rise sharply.
30 MINUTES+
Switches to 10-minute TTL. Engagement still registered but sampled less frequently. Velocity signal starts decaying.
VELOCITY VS VOLUME
50 replies in 5 minutes signals differently than 50 replies in 2 hours. Velocity of accumulation is the real signal, not total count.
§ 11 — Dwell Signals

Dwell Time — The 30-Second Rule

The model tracks both whether you paused and how long. But the curve maxes at exactly 30 seconds.

# Phoenix model — continuous dwell normalization
norm_scale: float = 30.0
normalized = clip(dwell_value, 0, 30) / 30.0

# 0s  → 0.0  (no signal)
# 15s → 0.5  (half signal)
# 30s → 1.0  (maximum signal)
# 60s → 1.0  (same as 30s — no additional benefit)

# Two separate signals tracked:
DWELL_WEIGHT           # binary: paused at all?
CONT_DWELL_TIME_WEIGHT # continuous: how long?
The Optimal Post Length

A post that takes 25-30 seconds to read and satisfies the reader earns the same dwell score as a 3-minute essay. The dwell ceiling is 30 seconds. Design for that — not more. Hook in the first line. Deliver in the body. Done at 30 seconds.

§ 11b — Click Signals

Click-Through Dwell Time

When someone clicks a link in your post, the algorithm tracks how long they spend on the destination page. This is a separate scored signal from post dwell — and most creators have never considered it.

# Phoenix model — click signal breakdown
click_score              # binary: did they click the link at all?
ContClickDwellTimeWeight # continuous: how long did they stay after clicking?

# These are two separate action scores — both tracked independently
# ContClickDwellTimeWeight applies the same normalization as post dwell
⚡
Your link destination quality is scored. If you link to a page that people immediately bounce from (click and close in 2 seconds), that generates a low click dwell score. If they stay and read for 20+ seconds, that's a high signal. Linking to low-quality, clickbait, or slow-loading pages actively hurts your post's score. Link only to content worth reading.
❌ Link Choices That Hurt
Clickbait headlines — visitors bounce immediately
Slow-loading pages — people abandon before reading
Paywalled content — reader leaves instantly
Landing pages with no real content
Links irrelevant to the post text
✅ Link Choices That Help
Long-form articles that match the post topic exactly
Your own content (blog, newsletter, thread)
Data / research people actually read through
Fast-loading pages with immediate value
Content that earns 20+ seconds of attention
§ 12 — Video Scoring

Video & The VQV Gate

Short videos lose an entire scoring category. Video only helps if you clear the minimum duration threshold.

# WeightedScorer — VQV eligibility check
fn vqv_weight_eligibility(candidate) -> f64 {
    if candidate.min_video_duration_ms > MIN_VIDEO_DURATION_MS {
        return VQV_WEIGHT  // full weight applied
    }
    return 0.0            // weight nulled out
}

# Also: QUOTED_VQV_WEIGHT for videos in quoted posts
# EnableQuotedVqvDurationCheck flag controls quoted video VQV
⚡
Binary gate: A video slightly below MIN_VIDEO_DURATION_MS gets zero VQV weight — not reduced weight. The entire scoring category is nulled. Commit to a length above the threshold or don't rely on video for VQV signal.

Video that qualifies for VQV earns signal on top of all other engagement actions — likes, replies, DMs, follows still all fire independently. A qualifying video with strong engagement is the highest-ceiling format in the algorithm when executed well.

§ 12b — Video Length Buckets

Video Duration Tiers

The algorithm doesn't just see "video" — it classifies every video into one of three discrete length buckets, each likely treated differently beyond just the VQV gate.

# tweet_type_metrics_hydrator.rs — video duration buckets

VIDEO_BT_10_60_SEC   # 10–60 seconds — may clear VQV gate
VIDEO_GT_60_SEC      # >60 seconds  — clearly above VQV gate
BucketDurationVQV WeightStrategy
Short <10s 0.0 (nulled) No VQV signal at all. Relies entirely on other action scores (likes, replies, DMs). Only worth posting if engagement quality compensates.
Mid 10–60s Conditional May clear MIN_VIDEO_DURATION_MS. This is the highest-risk zone — slightly below the threshold nulls VQV entirely. Know the exact cutoff before committing to this length.

💡
Practical rule: Never post a video in the 10–60s range without being certain it clears MIN_VIDEO_DURATION_MS. The safe strategy is to commit to videos clearly above 60 seconds, which guarantees VQV eligibility regardless of how the exact threshold is configured. Short-form clips under 10s should be treated as image-equivalent posts for scoring purposes — plan their engagement strategy accordingly.

§ 14 — Common Myths

Formatting, Lowercase, Articles, Communities

What the source code actually says about formatting choices — and which popular beliefs are myths.

MYTH: Lowercase Text Boosts Reach

Zero mention of letter case anywhere in the algorithm code. Phoenix represents posts as hash embeddings — it does not parse sentences. The "lowercase boosts reach" claim has no algorithmic basis. Letter case is completely invisible to the scoring system. Any effect is indirect: lowercase may feel more authentic to some audiences → better human engagement → indirect score improvement.

Articles / Long-form

No dedicated article boost in the scoring code. The product surface enum has 16 values but no special "article" multiplier. Long-form naturally earns more dwell time, and if the content converts readers to followers it's worth it. Otherwise a shorter post with equivalent engagement signals scores the same.

Communities

No dedicated weight multiplier for community posts in the scoring code. However, community audiences are pre-qualified for specific topics → naturally higher engagement rates → better ML predictions → higher predicted action probabilities. The real benefit: communities concentrate your engagement in a specific topic, strengthening your author embedding and improving For You routing for all your regular posts over time.

Line Breaks, Spacing, Emojis

Invisible to the scoring system. Affects human behavior → dwell and engagement → indirect score effect. Line breaks that make posts scannable cause readers to pause longer → dwell signal fires. Emojis have zero algorithmic value but may affect whether humans stop scrolling past your post.

The Banger System

§ 16 — The Gate

The Min Traction Gate

The most important mechanic most people don't know exists. The Banger classifier only runs on posts that already crossed a minimum engagement threshold.

Every published post → CONTENT_UNDERSTANDING_REALTIME_UNIFIED_POSTS ALL POSTS
After min traction → UNIFIED_POSTS_MIN_TRACTION_FOR_GROX BANGER SCREEN
Min traction + safety → UNIFIED_POSTS_MIN_TRACTION_FOR_GROX_PTOS SAFETY SCREEN
Min traction + media → UNIFIED_POSTS_MIN_TRACTION_FOR_GROX_MULTI_MODAL MULTIMODAL
🔑
The gate: Without crossing min traction, the banger classifier never sees your post. It doesn't matter how good your content is — if it doesn't get initial engagement, it never gets screened for viral amplification. The first 30-60 minutes are everything.

How to Cross the Threshold

  • DM the post to 5-10 people who will genuinely engage — fastest way to seed min traction without looking like spam
  • Get 2-3 real replies fast — reply weight is 2nd highest action weight, making early replies particularly valuable
  • Engage other posts immediately before posting — drives profile visits that convert to early views
  • Have your most engaged followers ready — they provide the cleanest traction signal for the algorithm

§ 18 — Vision Language Model

VLM — What It Is and Why It Affects You

VLM = Vision Language Model. It reads your image and text simultaneously and evaluates them as a single combined piece of content.

Regular language models only read text. The Grok VLM sees your image + caption + user profile all at once. It evaluates the combination — not the parts separately. This has major implications for how you use images.

✦ The Simple Version

Layman's Guide — Everything That Matters, Simply

Forget the code. Forget the jargon. Here's what the algorithm actually is, how to post, how to go viral, and how to grow from 100 to 10,000 — in plain English.

What The Algorithm Actually Is

X's algorithm is essentially one giant question machine. Every time someone opens their feed, it asks: "Which posts is this person most likely to follow the author of?" That's it. Not which posts will get the most likes. Not which posts are the most interesting. It ranks every single post by how likely a viewer is to tap "Follow" after seeing it.

It uses a machine learning model called Phoenix that has learned from billions of interactions what kind of post makes someone want to follow an account. It predicts 19 different things a person might do — follow, reply, retweet, DM it to a friend, like it, click your profile — and adds up the scores with weights. Following you is worth the most. Replying is second. DMing your post to someone is third. A simple like is mid-tier.

Your job is to make every post feel like the beginning of something worth following.

How to Post — The Simple Rules
📌
Post once a day, not five times
The algorithm penalises you every time you appear more than once in the same person's feed refresh. Your second post of the day only gets 94% of the score your first got. Your third gets 89%. Space posts at least 3 hours apart — ideally post your best once per day and make it count.
⏱️
The first 30 minutes are everything
The algorithm checks your engagement every 5 minutes for the first 30 minutes. How fast you get replies, likes, and reshares in that window determines whether your post spreads to people who don't follow you. Post when your most engaged followers are online — not when "the platform" is busiest. Stay active for 2 hours after posting and reply to every comment.
📏
Write for 25–30 seconds of reading time
The algorithm tracks how long someone pauses on your post. The score maxes out at 30 seconds — anything longer gets no extra credit. A post that takes 25 seconds to read and lands perfectly earns the same dwell score as a 3-minute essay. Write tight. Hook in the first line. Deliver the value. Done.
🎯
Stay on one topic — always
The algorithm builds a profile of what you talk about based on your last 128 posts. It then finds people who love that topic and routes your content to them. If you post about fitness, finance, and cooking, the model gets confused and can't confidently route you to anyone. Pick one lane and own it until you hit 10,000. You can expand later — not now.
🚫
Never go private — not even for a day
Private accounts are literally blocked from the viral content pipeline in the source code. The quality classifier that gives posts a viral boost doesn't even look at protected accounts. Going private to "clean up" your account cuts you off from every growth mechanic on the platform.
How to Go Viral — The Real Process

Going viral isn't luck. There's a literal system in the source code called BangerInitialScreen — a Grok AI that reads your post and gives it a quality score from 0 to 1. If it scores above 0.4, your post is flagged as high quality and gets pushed to strangers. If it scores below 0.4, it only reaches your existing followers. That score is permanent and assigned within the first hour.

1
Write something specific and original. The same AI that scores your post also detects "slop" — generic, AI-generated, or copy-paste content. Broad motivational posts, recycled advice, and vague takes all score low. The more specific and surprising your post, the higher it scores. A specific claim someone could disagree with beats a platitude everyone agrees with.
2
Seed the first 30–60 minutes. The viral AI only looks at your post if it already has some engagement — called the "min traction gate." No engagement = the AI never sees it = no viral boost. DM your post to 5–10 people who will genuinely engage. Get 2–3 real replies fast. This is not gaming the system — this is how the system is designed to work.
3
Use images that add information. The viral AI reads your images too. A screenshot of a chart, a dense infographic, or a photo that proves your point raises your quality score. A stock photo, a generic AI image, or an unrelated visual lowers it. Every image gets analysed alongside your text as one combined piece of content.
4
Post about what's trending — authentically. The AI is given the current trending topics every hour. It checks if your post is relevant to them. Posts that connect to a trending topic get boosted into that topic's feed on top of the regular For You feed. You get two distribution channels instead of one. Don't force it — but if what you post naturally connects to something trending, say so explicitly.
5
Make it quotable and DM-worthy. Replies are great. But quotes and DM shares reach entirely new audiences. A quote brings someone's whole follower list to your post. A DM share means someone thought "I need to send this to my friend" — and DM shares are scored higher than likes. Design every post with the question: "Would someone DM this to someone they know?"
⏰
The 80-hour deadline. Every post has exactly 80 hours of life in the algorithm. After that, it's gone — it will never appear in anyone's For You feed again, ever. There is no "it blew up a week later" on X. Everything — all seeding, engagement, sharing — must happen within those 80 hours. The first 6 hours are the most important.
How to Increase Reach & Engagement
💬
Reply to big accounts — it's a growth hack hidden in plain sight
When you reply to a large account, your reply becomes its own ranked post in other people's feeds. A sharp, insightful reply to a 500K account can get 50K impressions for an account with 200 followers. This is the fastest out-of-network exposure available to small accounts. Do it every day.

Engage where your audience hangs out
The algorithm measures how many followers you and a viewer share in common. The more accounts you both follow, the higher your posts rank in their feed — even before they follow you. New accounts have an advantage — use it immediately
New accounts get a higher out-of-network reach bonus and are placed in a special discovery mode that routes them to topic-based audiences from day one.
